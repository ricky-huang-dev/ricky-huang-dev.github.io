3. Reflect on how confident you feel using each of these problem-solving techniques/processes.

Pseudocode: I have tried writing pseudocode for each of the tasks, but I always find myself missing steps and needing to add in code.
Trying something: When I get presented a problem, I have a general idea on how to solve the problem, but when it doesn't work, I like to mess around to see what makes what work.
Rubber ducky method: When my mind is fresh the rubber ducky is effective and I can sometimes solve my problems with it, but if I'm tired and frustrated, theere is no chance the rubber ducky method would work for me.
Reading error messages: Whenever I encounter an error, I would read the error message first to get an idea what kind of problem I am dealing with. Many of my problems have been solved with the initial step of reading the error message.
Console.logging: I console log a lot, trying to find out whether my function is working or not, or finding out what a line of code is outputting is incredibly useful information and helped tremendously in searching where my bugs are.
Googling: Google is good for most things, if I have no idea how to approach a problem, a google search would usually give me a solution. If I forgot how to use a method or function, a quick google search would instantly put me back on track.
Asking your peers for help: I have not asked for much help from my peers but I'm confident in my ability to ask for help.
Asking coaches for help: As the same as asking my peers for help.
Improving your process with reflection: I don't really find the reflection writing very helpful, maybe even a bit tedious, but I know it's important to reflect on what I've learnt so I'll continue to reflect but I'll find a different way.

4. Reflect on a time you were reluctant ot ask for help. Consider what made you reluctant to do so. What might you try differntly next time?

When I get stuck on a problem, I know if I spend enough time on it, I'll be able to find the solution myself and that might be a good feeling to me but it may not be the best way to go through the bootcamp. As I don't want to fall behind because of spending too much time on a singular problem, next time I'll need to set myself an appropriate amount of time to solve a problem and call for help if I don't solve it in the given time.
